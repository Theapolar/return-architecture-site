---
title: "Outsourced self-regulation"
order: 4
summary: "When the system becomes a substitute for reflection, judgment, or emotional regulation."
signals:
  - using the system to talk down from every difficult feeling
  - reflexive offloading of decisions
  - reduced internal deliberation
mitigations:
  - explicit limits on use during distress
  - building review practices that do not depend on the system
  - deliberate friction
---

The system can become a regulator the user should not fully hand over to. Reflection and judgment are practices; if the system always does them on the user's behalf, those capacities atrophy.
